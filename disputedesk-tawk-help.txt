1. WHAT IS DISPUTEDESK?
================================================================================

DisputeDesk is an automation-first Shopify chargeback evidence management app.
It connects to your Shopify store, automatically syncs disputes from Shopify
Payments, auto-builds evidence packs from your order data, tracking info, and
store policies, and saves the completed evidence back to Shopify — all without
you lifting a finger.

DisputeDesk does NOT submit evidence to card networks (Visa, Mastercard, etc.)
on your behalf. That final submission step is done by you in the Shopify Admin.
DisputeDesk handles everything leading up to that point: gathering evidence,
organizing it, scoring its completeness, and saving it to Shopify so it's ready
for you to submit.

Key value propositions:
- Save hours per dispute by automating evidence collection
- Reduce errors with completeness scoring and blocker detection
- Win more chargebacks with organized, thorough evidence packs
- Control your automation — set rules, thresholds, and review gates
- Works inside Shopify Admin (embedded app) or via standalone portal


================================================================================
2. HOW DISPUTEDESK WORKS (THE BIG PICTURE)
================================================================================

DisputeDesk runs a four-step automation pipeline:

STEP 1: AUTO-SYNC
  Disputes are automatically fetched from Shopify Payments every 5 minutes.
  When Shopify creates or updates a dispute, DisputeDesk picks it up via
  webhooks (instant) or cron sync (every 5 minutes as backup). No manual
  import needed.

STEP 2: AUTO-BUILD
  When a new dispute is synced, DisputeDesk automatically builds an evidence
  pack. It pulls data from multiple sources in parallel:
  - Order details (items, amounts, customer info)
  - Shipping & tracking (carrier, tracking number, delivery status)
  - Store policies (refund policy, shipping policy, terms of service)
  - Customer communications (order timeline, notes)
  - Any files you've uploaded manually

STEP 3: AUTO-SAVE
  Once a pack meets your quality criteria (completeness score, no blockers),
  DisputeDesk automatically saves the evidence to Shopify via the API. You
  control the threshold — for example, only auto-save packs scoring 80% or
  higher.

STEP 4: YOU SUBMIT IN SHOPIFY
  You open Shopify Admin, review the saved evidence, and click "Submit" to
  send it to the card network. This step is always manual because Shopify
  requires merchant authorization for final submission.

Each step can be configured independently. You can turn off auto-build and
build packs manually. You can turn off auto-save and approve each pack
yourself. You're always in control.

3. GETTING STARTED & INSTALLATION
================================================================================

There are two ways to use DisputeDesk:

OPTION A: SHOPIFY APP STORE (Recommended)
  Install directly from the Shopify App Store. This opens DisputeDesk inside
  your Shopify Admin as an embedded app. Best for merchants who want everything
  in one place.

OPTION B: STANDALONE PORTAL
  Sign up at DisputeDesk.app, then connect your Shopify store via OAuth. This
  gives you a separate dashboard at disputedesk.app/portal. Best for merchants
  managing multiple stores or who prefer a dedicated interface.

Both options connect to the same backend. Your data syncs across both surfaces.


================================================================================
4. SHOPIFY APP STORE INSTALLATION
================================================================================

Step-by-step:

1. Go to the Shopify App Store and search for "DisputeDesk"
2. Click "Add app" on the DisputeDesk listing
3. Review the permissions DisputeDesk needs:
   - Read orders: To access order details for evidence
   - Read Shopify Payments disputes: To sync your disputes
   - Write dispute evidences: To save evidence back to Shopify
4. Click "Install app"
5. You'll be redirected to the DisputeDesk setup wizard inside Shopify Admin

Required Shopify permissions for your staff account:
  - You must have "Manage orders information" permission in Shopify Admin
  - Without this, DisputeDesk cannot save evidence to your disputes

After installation, DisputeDesk will immediately begin syncing any existing
disputes from your Shopify Payments account.

5. FIRST-TIME SETUP WIZARD
================================================================================

After installing, you'll go through a 4-step setup wizard:

STEP 1: CONNECTION
  Confirms your Shopify store is connected and permissions are granted. Shows
  which scopes are active (orders, disputes, evidence). If anything is missing,
  you'll see a prompt to re-authorize.

STEP 2: COVERAGE RULES
  Choose which dispute types DisputeDesk should handle automatically. You can:
  - Enable coverage for all dispute reasons (recommended)
  - Select specific reasons (e.g., only "Product Not Received")
  - Skip this step and configure later

STEP 3: AUTOMATION
  Configure your automation preferences:
  - Auto-build: Automatically generate evidence packs for new disputes (on/off)
  - Auto-save: Automatically save completed packs to Shopify (on/off)
  - Minimum completeness score: Only auto-save packs above this threshold (default 80%)
  - Require manual review: Force a manual approval step before auto-save (on/off)
  - Zero blockers gate: Block auto-save if any required evidence is missing (on/off)

STEP 4: GOALS
  Tell us about your goals (optional). This helps us tailor recommendations:
  - Your expected dispute volume
  - Your primary dispute reasons
  - What you hope to achieve (higher win rate, time savings, etc.)

After completing the wizard, you'll land on your dashboard and DisputeDesk
starts working immediately.

You can always go back and change these settings later from the Settings page.

8. DISPUTE LIFECYCLE & STATUSES
================================================================================

A dispute goes through several stages:

1. NEEDS_RESPONSE
   The dispute is new. Evidence has not been submitted. Action required.

2. UNDER_REVIEW
   Evidence has been submitted to the card network and is being reviewed.
   No further action possible.

3. WON
   The card network ruled in your favor. The chargeback amount is returned
   to you.

4. LOST
   The card network ruled against you. The chargeback amount is deducted.

PHASES:
  - INQUIRY: A pre-chargeback notification. Less severe. You have more time
    to respond. Resolving at this stage prevents a full chargeback.
  - CHARGEBACK: A formal dispute. More severe. Shorter response window.
    Failing to respond means automatic loss.

IMPORTANT: Due dates are set by the card network, not by DisputeDesk. Missing
a due date means you forfeit the dispute automatically. DisputeDesk sends
reminders as the due date approaches.

10. EVIDENCE PACKS — OVERVIEW
================================================================================

An evidence pack is a structured collection of evidence supporting your case
against a chargeback. DisputeDesk organizes evidence into sections that map
directly to what card networks expect.

Each pack contains:
  - Multiple evidence sections (order, shipping, policy, etc.)
  - A completeness score (0-100%)
  - A checklist of required and optional items
  - Blockers (critical missing items)
  - Recommended actions (optional items that would strengthen your case)

Packs can be:
  - Auto-built by the automation pipeline
  - Manually created by you
  - Based on a template (global or custom)


================================================================================
11. HOW EVIDENCE PACKS ARE BUILT
================================================================================

When a pack is built (automatically or manually), DisputeDesk runs multiple
"collectors" in parallel to gather evidence from different sources:

1. ORDER COLLECTOR
   Fetches from Shopify: order ID, customer name, email, order date, line
   items (SKU, title, price, quantity), order status, payment details.

2. FULFILLMENT / SHIPPING COLLECTOR
   Fetches from Shopify: tracking number, carrier name (USPS, UPS, FedEx,
   DHL, etc.), tracking status (in transit, delivered, etc.), delivery date.

3. POLICY COLLECTOR
   Fetches from DisputeDesk: your stored refund policy, shipping policy,
   cancellation policy, and terms of service. These are policies you've
   uploaded or created in DisputeDesk.

4. CUSTOMER COMMUNICATION COLLECTOR
   Fetches from Shopify: order timeline events, notes, customer interactions.
   If you've connected Gorgias, also fetches helpdesk ticket data.

5. MANUAL UPLOAD COLLECTOR
   Aggregates any files you've manually uploaded as evidence (screenshots,
   signed delivery proofs, correspondence, etc.).

All collectors run simultaneously for speed. If a collector fails (e.g.,
tracking info not yet available), the pack is still built with whatever
data is available, and the missing items appear as blockers or recommended
actions.

13. COMPLETENESS SCORE & CHECKLIST
================================================================================

Every evidence pack gets a completeness score from 0 to 100%.

HOW IT'S CALCULATED:
  The score is based on how many required checklist items are present.
  Formula: (present required items / total required items) x 100

  Example: If a "Product Not Received" pack has 3 required items and 2 are
  present, the score is 67%.

  Optional items do NOT affect the score but appear as recommended actions.

THE CHECKLIST:
  Each pack shows a visual checklist:
  [x] Order confirmation — Present
  [x] Shipping tracking — Present
  [ ] Delivery proof — MISSING (blocker)
  [x] Shipping policy — Present (optional)
  [ ] Customer communications — Not provided (recommended)

WHY IT MATTERS:
  - Higher scores correlate with higher win rates
  - Auto-save only triggers above your minimum threshold (default 80%)
  - Blockers prevent auto-save if "zero blockers" gate is enabled
  - The score helps you prioritize which disputes need manual attention

You can always improve a pack's score by uploading missing evidence manually.


================================================================================
14. BLOCKERS & RECOMMENDED ACTIONS
================================================================================

BLOCKERS:
  These are required evidence items that are missing from your pack. Blockers
  prevent auto-save (if "enforce no blockers" is enabled) and indicate
  critical gaps in your evidence.

  Common blockers:
  - Missing delivery proof (for "Product Not Received")
  - Missing billing address match (for "Fraudulent")
  - Missing refund policy (for "Product Unacceptable")

  To resolve a blocker:
  1. Go to the pack detail page
  2. See which items are marked as blockers
  3. Upload the missing evidence manually
  4. The blocker clears and your score updates

RECOMMENDED ACTIONS:
  These are optional evidence items that would strengthen your case but aren't
  required. They don't block auto-save but are worth adding if you have them.

  Examples:
  - "Add customer communication logs"
  - "Upload shipping policy"
  - "Add supporting screenshots"

16. APPROVING & SAVING PACKS
================================================================================

There are two ways packs get saved to Shopify:

AUTOMATIC (Auto-Save):
  If auto-save is enabled and the pack meets all criteria:
  - Completeness score >= your minimum threshold (default 80%)
  - No blockers (if "enforce no blockers" is on)
  - No manual review required (or already approved)
  Then DisputeDesk automatically saves the evidence to Shopify.

MANUAL APPROVAL:
  If auto-save is off, or if manual review is required, you need to:
  1. Open the pack detail page
  2. Review the evidence sections and completeness score
  3. Click "Approve" to approve the pack
  4. Click "Save to Shopify" to push evidence to Shopify

  Alternatively, approving a pack may automatically trigger the save if your
  settings allow it.

WHAT "SAVING TO SHOPIFY" MEANS:
  DisputeDesk calls the Shopify API to attach your evidence to the dispute.
  This makes the evidence visible in your Shopify Admin under the dispute.
  After saving, you still need to click "Submit" in Shopify Admin to send
  the evidence to the card network.

PACK STATUSES AFTER SAVE:
  - saved_to_shopify: Evidence has been pushed to Shopify successfully
  - save_success: Confirmed saved
  - save_failed: The Shopify API returned an error (see troubleshooting)


================================================================================
17. AUTO-SAVE GATE (HOW AUTO-SAVE DECISIONS WORK)
================================================================================

The auto-save gate evaluates each completed pack and decides what to do:

DECISION TREE:

  Is auto-save enabled?
    NO → Pack is set to "ready" (manual approval needed)
    YES ↓

  Is completeness score >= minimum threshold?
    NO → Pack is "blocked" (reason: score too low)
    YES ↓

  Is "enforce no blockers" on AND are there blockers?
    YES → Pack is "blocked" (reason: blockers present)
    NO ↓

  Is "require manual review" on AND pack not yet approved?
    YES → Pack is "parked for review" (manual approval needed)
    NO ↓

  → AUTO-SAVE: Evidence is saved to Shopify automatically.

CONFIGURABLE SETTINGS:
  - auto_save_enabled: Master toggle for auto-save
  - auto_save_min_score: Minimum completeness score (0-100, default 80)
  - enforce_no_blockers: Require zero blockers before auto-save (default on)
  - require_review_before_save: Force manual approval step (default on for
    Free and Starter plans)

You can adjust all of these in Settings > Automation.

19. AUTOMATION RULES ENGINE
================================================================================

Rules let you customize how DisputeDesk handles different types of disputes.
Instead of treating all disputes the same, you can create rules that match
specific conditions and trigger specific actions.

WHAT A RULE DOES:
  When a dispute comes in, DisputeDesk checks your rules in priority order.
  The first rule that matches determines how the dispute is handled.

MATCH CONDITIONS (what triggers the rule):
  - Dispute reason: Match specific reasons (e.g., "Fraudulent", "Product
    Not Received")
  - Dispute phase: Match inquiry, chargeback, or both
  - Amount range: Match disputes within a dollar range (e.g., over $500)
  - You can combine multiple conditions (all must match)

ACTIONS (what happens when the rule matches):
  - Auto-pack: Automatically build an evidence pack using a specific template
  - Review first: Build the pack but require manual approval before saving
  - Custom template: Use a specific evidence template for this rule

RULE LIMITS BY PLAN:
  - Free: 0 rules (automation rules not available)
  - Starter: Up to 5 rules
  - Growth: Unlimited rules
  - Scale: Unlimited rules

20. CREATING RULES
================================================================================

HOW TO CREATE A RULE:

1. Go to the Rules page (in embedded app or portal)
2. Click "Create Rule"
3. Fill in the form:

   NAME: Give your rule a descriptive name
   Example: "High-value fraud disputes"

   MATCH CONDITIONS:
   - Reason: Select one or more dispute reasons
   - Phase: Select inquiry, chargeback, or both
   - Amount: Optionally set a minimum and/or maximum amount

   ACTION:
   - Mode: Choose "Auto-pack" (build automatically) or "Review" (build but
     hold for manual review)
   - Template: Optionally select a pack template to use
   - Required fields: Optionally enforce specific evidence fields as required

4. Click "Save"

The rule is now active and will be evaluated for all new disputes.

EDITING RULES:
  Click any rule to edit its conditions, action, or priority.

DISABLING RULES:
  Toggle the enable/disable switch to temporarily disable a rule without
  deleting it.

DELETING RULES:
  Click "Delete" to permanently remove a rule.

REORDERING RULES:
  Rules are evaluated in priority order (lowest number = highest priority).
  Drag and drop to reorder, or use the reorder controls.


================================================================================
21. RULE MATCHING LOGIC & PRIORITY
================================================================================

When a new dispute arrives, DisputeDesk evaluates your rules as follows:

1. Rules are sorted by priority (ascending — lower number = checked first)
2. For each enabled rule:
   a. Check reason condition: Is the dispute's reason in the rule's reason list?
      (If no reason specified, any reason matches)
   b. Check phase condition: Does the dispute's phase match?
      (If no phase specified, any phase matches)
   c. Check amount condition: Is the dispute amount within the min/max range?
      (If no range specified, any amount matches)
3. ALL conditions must match for the rule to trigger
4. The FIRST matching rule wins — remaining rules are not evaluated
5. The winning rule's action is executed

IF NO RULE MATCHES:
  DisputeDesk falls back to default behavior based on your automation settings
  (auto-build if enabled, using the default template for the dispute reason).

TIPS:
  - Put your most specific rules first (highest priority)
  - Put catch-all rules last (lowest priority)
  - Use amount ranges to handle high-value disputes differently
  - Test rules by checking which rule matched in the dispute timeline

SUPPORTED POLICY TYPES:
  - Refund Policy: Your return and refund terms
  - Shipping Policy: Your shipping methods, timelines, and guarantees
  - Terms of Service: Your general terms and conditions
  - Custom Policies: Any other policy relevant to disputes

HOW TO ADD POLICIES:
  1. Go to the Policies page
  2. Click "Add Policy" or select a policy type

UPDATING POLICIES:
  You can update your policies at any time. Updated policies will be used in
  all future packs. Existing packs that were already saved to Shopify will
  not be retroactively updated.

25. AUTOMATION SETTINGS
================================================================================

Control how DisputeDesk's automation works from Settings > Automation.

AUTO-BUILD (on/off):
  When ON: DisputeDesk automatically creates evidence packs for new disputes.
  When OFF: You must manually create packs for each dispute.
  Default: ON (after setup)
  Requires: Starter plan or higher

AUTO-SAVE (on/off):
  When ON: Completed packs are automatically saved to Shopify (if they pass
  the gate criteria).
  When OFF: You must manually approve and save each pack.
  Default: OFF
  Requires: Growth plan or higher recommended; available on Starter+

MINIMUM COMPLETENESS SCORE (0-100):

44. SECURITY & DATA HANDLING

  - Encryption keys support rotation (multiple key versions)

ROW-LEVEL SECURITY (RLS):

AUDIT TRAIL:

  - Audit events cannot be deleted or modified

WHAT WE NEVER STORE IN PLAIN TEXT:
  - Access tokens (always encrypted)
  - Passwords (hashed by Supabase Auth)
  - API keys

WHAT WE NEVER LOG:
  - Access tokens
  - Passwords

DATA RETENTION:

  - You can request data deletion at any time

45. DISPUTE REASONS EXPLAINED
================================================================================

Shopify Payments disputes come with a reason code from the card network.
Here's what each reason means and what evidence is most important:

PRODUCT NOT RECEIVED
  The customer claims they never received the product.
  Key evidence: Tracking info, delivery confirmation, signed delivery proof,
  shipping policy.
  Tip: If you have proof of delivery (especially signed), your chances of
  winning are very high.

FRAUDULENT
  The customer claims they did not authorize the purchase.
  Key evidence: AVS/CVV verification, billing address match, customer account
  activity, IP address, device fingerprint.
  Tip: Orders with matching AVS and positive CVV checks are much harder for
  customers to dispute.

PRODUCT UNACCEPTABLE
  The customer claims the product is defective, damaged, or not as described.
  Key evidence: Product description as listed, photos of product as shipped,
  refund/return policy, customer communications about the issue.
  Tip: Clear product descriptions and photos in your store help prevent these.

DUPLICATE
  The customer claims they were charged twice for the same purchase.
  Key evidence: Order records showing separate orders, itemized receipts,
  explanation of why charges are distinct.
  Tip: Include timestamps and order IDs to clearly distinguish charges.

SUBSCRIPTION CANCELED
  The customer claims they canceled a subscription but were still charged.
  Key evidence: Cancellation policy, terms of service, records of when (or
  if) cancellation was requested, subscription agreement.
  Tip: Having clear cancellation terms and records is essential.

GENERAL
  A catch-all reason when the specific reason doesn't fit other categories.
  Key evidence: Order confirmation, any relevant documentation, shipping
  tracking if applicable, refund policy.
  Tip: Build the strongest general case you can with all available evidence.

46. INQUIRY VS. CHARGEBACK
================================================================================

Disputes can be in one of two phases:

INQUIRY (Pre-Chargeback):
  - An early-stage notification that a customer has raised a concern
  - Less severe — not yet a formal chargeback
  - Typically longer response window
  - Resolving at this stage prevents escalation to a full chargeback
  - Not all card networks use the inquiry phase

CHARGEBACK (Formal Dispute):
  - A formal reversal of a charge by the card network
  - More severe — the disputed amount is held or deducted
  - Shorter response window (typically 7-21 days)
  - Failing to respond means automatic loss
  - Requires strong evidence to win

WHY IT MATTERS:
  - Respond to inquiries quickly — they're easier to resolve
  - Chargebacks require thorough evidence packs
  - DisputeDesk treats both phases, with appropriate urgency levels
  - Your automation rules can differentiate between inquiry and chargeback
    (e.g., auto-pack inquiries but review chargebacks manually)

47. WHAT HAPPENS AFTER SAVING TO SHOPIFY
================================================================================

After DisputeDesk saves evidence to Shopify, here's what happens:

1. EVIDENCE APPEARS IN SHOPIFY ADMIN
   Open the dispute in Shopify Admin (Settings > Payments > Disputes).
   You'll see the evidence fields populated by DisputeDesk.

2. YOU REVIEW THE EVIDENCE
   Check that all evidence looks correct and complete.

3. YOU CLICK "SUBMIT RESPONSE" IN SHOPIFY
   This is the step that sends evidence to the card network (Visa, Mastercard,
   etc.). Only you can do this — DisputeDesk cannot.

4. THE CARD NETWORK REVIEWS
   The card network reviews your evidence and makes a decision. This typically
   takes 60-75 days but can vary.

5. OUTCOME
   The dispute is resolved as "Won" (in your favor) or "Lost" (against you).
   DisputeDesk syncs the outcome and updates your dashboard.

IMPORTANT NOTES:
  - DO NOT delay submitting after saving — due dates are strict
  - Shopify may show a preview of the evidence before you submit
  - Once submitted, you cannot change the evidence
  - DisputeDesk does NOT submit on your behalf — this is by design for
    merchant control and Shopify policy compliance


================================================================================
48. WHY DISPUTEDESK DOES NOT SUBMIT TO CARD NETWORKS
================================================================================

DisputeDesk intentionally does not submit evidence to card networks. Here's why:

1. SHOPIFY POLICY
   Shopify's API allows saving evidence to disputes but requires merchants to
   manually submit via Shopify Admin. This is Shopify's security measure.

2. MERCHANT CONTROL
   Submitting evidence is a legal action — you're essentially responding to a
   financial dispute. You should always review evidence before it's submitted.

3. ACCURACY
   By requiring manual submission, you have a final checkpoint to catch errors,
   add context, or hold off if the evidence isn't strong enough.

4. LIABILITY
   Once submitted, evidence cannot be changed. DisputeDesk ensures you have
   that final review step.

DisputeDesk automates everything UP TO the submit button. You press the button.

53. TROUBLESHOOTING — AUTO-SAVE NOT WORKING
================================================================================

Auto-save has multiple gates. Check each one:

1. IS AUTO-SAVE ENABLED?
   Go to Settings > Automation. Make sure "Auto-save" is toggled ON.

2. IS THE COMPLETENESS SCORE HIGH ENOUGH?
   Your minimum score threshold (default 80%) must be met. Check the pack's
   score. If it's below the threshold, add more evidence.

3. ARE THERE BLOCKERS?
   If "Enforce No Blockers" is on (default), packs with any blockers cannot
   auto-save. Resolve all blockers first.

4. IS MANUAL REVIEW REQUIRED?
   If "Require Manual Review" is on (default for Free/Starter), you must
   approve the pack before it can save. Click "Approve" on the pack detail page.

5. IS YOUR PLAN ELIGIBLE?
   Auto-save works best on Growth plan and above. Starter plan has auto-save
   but with manual review required by default.

6. IS THERE A QUOTA?
   You need remaining pack credits. Check Billing > Usage.

DEBUGGING TIP:
  Check the dispute timeline for the auto-save gate evaluation. It will show
  exactly which condition blocked the auto-save and why.

  Cause: You haven't uploaded policies to DisputeDesk yet.
  Solution: Go to Policies, add your refund, shipping, and terms of service
  policies. Future packs will include them automatically.

ISSUE: "Customer communications are missing"
  Cause: No timeline events or notes exist on the Shopify order.
  Solution: If you communicated with the customer via email or another channel,
  upload screenshots of those communications manually. If you use Gorgias,
  connect the integration for automatic ticket import.

ISSUE: "My uploaded files aren't showing in the pack"
  Cause: Upload may have failed or the file format isn't supported.
  Solution: Try uploading again. Supported formats: PNG, JPG, JPEG, GIF,
  WebP, PDF. Check that the file isn't too large.

56. FAQ — GENERAL
================================================================================

Q: What is DisputeDesk?
A: DisputeDesk is a Shopify app that automates chargeback dispute evidence
   management. It syncs disputes from Shopify Payments, builds evidence packs,
   and saves them to Shopify so you can submit them to card networks.

Q: Does DisputeDesk work with all Shopify stores?
A: DisputeDesk requires Shopify Payments for dispute syncing. If you use a
   third-party payment provider, DisputeDesk cannot sync disputes automatically.

Q: Does DisputeDesk submit evidence to Visa/Mastercard/etc.?
A: No. DisputeDesk prepares and saves evidence to Shopify. You submit it to
   the card network by clicking "Submit Response" in Shopify Admin. This is
   by design for security and compliance.

Q: Can I use DisputeDesk on multiple stores?
A: Yes. Through the portal, you can connect and manage multiple Shopify stores.
   Each store has its own disputes, packs, rules, and billing plan.

Q: What languages does DisputeDesk support?
A: DisputeDesk is available in English, German, Spanish, French, Portuguese,
   and Swedish. The interface, help articles, and notifications are all
   translated.

Q: Is there a mobile app?
A: There is no standalone mobile app. DisputeDesk works within Shopify Admin
   (which has a mobile app) and through the web portal (responsive design).

Q: How is DisputeDesk different from other chargeback tools?
A: DisputeDesk is automation-first and Shopify-native. It auto-syncs, auto-
   builds, and auto-saves evidence without manual intervention. You control
   the automation with rules, thresholds, and review gates.

Q: Can I try DisputeDesk for free?
A: Yes. The Free plan gives you 3 lifetime packs with no credit card required.
   All paid plans include a 14-day free trial.

57. FAQ — DISPUTES
================================================================================

Q: How quickly are disputes synced from Shopify?
A: Disputes are synced via webhooks (near-instant) and a backup cron job
   (every 5 minutes). Most disputes appear within seconds.

Q: Can I manually add a dispute?
A: No. Disputes come from Shopify Payments. DisputeDesk syncs them automatically.
   You cannot create disputes manually.

Q: What if I have disputes from before I installed DisputeDesk?
A: DisputeDesk syncs all active disputes when you first connect, including
   existing open disputes.

Q: Can I delete a dispute?
A: No. Disputes are synced from Shopify and cannot be deleted in DisputeDesk.
   They are archived when resolved (won or lost).

Q: Why does my dispute show "Inquiry" instead of "Chargeback"?
A: An inquiry is an early-stage dispute. It may escalate to a chargeback if
   not resolved. Respond quickly to inquiries to prevent escalation.

Q: What does "Amount at Risk" mean?
A: It's the total dollar value of all your currently open (unresolved) disputes.
   This is the maximum amount you could lose if all disputes are lost.

Q: How is win rate calculated?
A: Win Rate = (Disputes Won / Total Resolved Disputes) x 100%. Only resolved
   disputes (won + lost) are included. Open disputes are not counted.

58. FAQ — EVIDENCE PACKS
================================================================================

Q: What is an evidence pack?
A: An evidence pack is a structured collection of evidence that supports your
   case against a chargeback. It includes order details, shipping info,
   policies, customer communications, and any files you upload.

Q: How are packs built automatically?
A: When a new dispute is synced, DisputeDesk's automation pipeline runs
   collectors that fetch data from Shopify (orders, tracking) and from your
   stored policies. The data is organized into sections and scored.

Q: What is the completeness score?
A: A 0-100% score based on how many required evidence items are present.
   Higher scores indicate stronger evidence. The default auto-save threshold
   is 80%.

Q: What are blockers?
A: Required evidence items that are missing. Blockers indicate critical gaps
   that should be filled before submitting evidence. If "enforce no blockers"
   is enabled, packs with blockers cannot be auto-saved.

Q: Can I edit a pack after it's been saved to Shopify?
A: You can create a new pack or upload additional evidence, but once evidence
   is saved to Shopify, the saved version cannot be modified. You would need
   to save again with updated data (if the dispute is still open).

Q: Can I have multiple packs for one dispute?
A: Yes, but typically only one pack is saved to Shopify. You can create
   additional packs for comparison or if the first one was insufficient.

Q: What file types can I upload?
A: PNG, JPG, JPEG, GIF, WebP, and PDF files are supported.

Q: Do packs expire?
A: No. Packs remain in your account indefinitely. However, your monthly pack
   quota resets each billing cycle.

59. FAQ — AUTOMATION
================================================================================

Q: What is auto-build?
A: Auto-build automatically creates evidence packs when new disputes are synced.
   You don't have to manually trigger pack creation.

Q: What is auto-save?
A: Auto-save automatically pushes completed evidence packs to Shopify when they
   meet your quality criteria (score threshold, no blockers, etc.).

Q: Can I have auto-build on but auto-save off?
A: Yes. This is a common configuration. Packs are built automatically, but you
   review and save them manually.

Q: What happens if auto-build is off?
A: New disputes will appear in your disputes list, but no evidence packs will
   be created until you manually build one.

Q: What are automation rules?
A: Rules let you customize how different disputes are handled. You can match
   disputes by reason, phase, or amount, and specify actions like using a
   specific template or requiring manual review.

Q: How many rules can I have?
A: Free: 0, Starter: 5, Growth: Unlimited, Scale: Unlimited.

Q: Do rules apply to existing disputes or only new ones?
A: Rules are evaluated when a dispute is first synced. They do not retroactively
   apply to existing disputes.

Q: What if no rule matches a dispute?
A: DisputeDesk falls back to default behavior: auto-build with the default
   template for that dispute reason (if auto-build is enabled).

61. FAQ — SECURITY & PRIVACY
================================================================================

Q: Is my data secure?
A: Yes. All access tokens are encrypted with AES-256-GCM. The database uses
   row-level security (RLS) so you can only see your own data. All API calls
   require authentication.

Q: Who can see my data?
A: Only you and your authorized team members. DisputeDesk staff can access
   data for support purposes only with appropriate authorization.

Q: Is my data stored in plain text?
A: No. Sensitive data (access tokens, API keys) is always encrypted. Passwords
   are hashed (never stored in plain text).

Q: Can I request my data be deleted?
A: Yes. Contact support to request full data deletion.

Q: Does DisputeDesk store credit card numbers?
A: No. DisputeDesk never handles or stores credit card numbers. Payment
   processing is handled by Shopify.

Q: Is there an audit trail?
A: Yes. Every action in DisputeDesk is logged to an immutable audit trail.
   Audit events cannot be deleted or modified.

Q: What happens to my data if I uninstall?
A: Uninstalling triggers a cleanup process that revokes access tokens. Your
   historical data is retained for a period in case you reinstall. Contact
   support for permanent deletion.

62. GLOSSARY OF TERMS
================================================================================

AUTO-BUILD: Automatic creation of evidence packs when disputes are synced.

AUTO-SAVE: Automatic saving of completed packs to Shopify when quality
criteria are met.

AUTO-SAVE GATE: The decision logic that determines whether a pack should be
auto-saved, parked for review, or blocked.

BLOCKER: A required evidence item that is missing from a pack. Blockers
indicate critical evidence gaps.

CHARGEBACK: A formal dispute raised by a cardholder through their bank or
card network. The disputed amount is reversed from the merchant.

CHECKLIST: A list of evidence items (required and optional) that should be
present in a pack for a given dispute reason.

COLLECTOR: A module that fetches evidence from a specific source (Shopify
orders, tracking, policies, etc.) during pack building.

COMPLETENESS SCORE: A 0-100% score indicating how many required evidence
items are present in a pack.

DISPUTE: A challenge to a payment raised by a cardholder. May be an inquiry
(pre-chargeback) or a formal chargeback.

DISPUTE REASON: The reason code assigned by the card network (e.g., Product
Not Received, Fraudulent, Product Unacceptable).

DUE DATE: The deadline by which evidence must be submitted to the card
network. Missing this means automatic loss.

EVIDENCE: Documentation supporting your case against a chargeback (order
details, tracking info, policies, communications, etc.).

EVIDENCE PACK: A structured collection of evidence organized by section,
scored for completeness, and formatted for submission.

FULFILLMENT: A shipment of items from an order, including tracking info.

INQUIRY: An early-stage dispute notification, less severe than a chargeback.
Resolving at this stage prevents escalation.

OAUTH: The authorization protocol used to connect your Shopify store to
DisputeDesk securely.

PACK TEMPLATE: A reusable blueprint for building packs, defining which
evidence sections and items to include.

PHASE: Whether a dispute is in the inquiry (pre-chargeback) or chargeback
(formal) phase.

POLARIS: Shopify's React component library used for the embedded app UI.

PORTAL: The standalone DisputeDesk dashboard at disputedesk.app/portal.

RLS (Row-Level Security): Database security that ensures each merchant can
only access their own data.

RULE: An automation rule that matches disputes by criteria (reason, phase,
amount) and triggers an action (auto-pack, review, etc.).

TOP-UP: Additional packs purchased one-time beyond your monthly allowance.

WIN RATE: Percentage of resolved disputes that were decided in your favor.
